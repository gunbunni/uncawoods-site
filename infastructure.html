UNC Asheville is facing serious financial and infrastructure challenges, but clearing 45 acres of the South Campus woods to build a $250 million stadium and mixed-use complex is not the right solution.

The woods are not just unused land. It provides real ecological, educational, and community value. They also reflect UNCA’s commitments to:

  • Sustainability
  • Carbon neutrality
  • Academic excellence

Protecting this forest is important, and there are better ways for UNCA to grow without losing such a valuable resource!

1. Use Land That’s Already Cleared or Underutilized

  • Focus development on the 22-acre “Future Building Site” near Broadway, previously prepared for The Health Adventure museum.
  • Consider unused parking lots, athletic fields with expansion potential, or other disturbed areas for development instead of the forest.

2. Build Smarter Housing

  • Expand the existing residence halls upward instead of cutting down trees.
  • Add extra stories for affordable, high-density housing while keeping green space intact.
  • Use public-private partnerships to fund upgrades while keeping housing affordable and the woods protected.

3. Generate Revenue Without Destroying the Forest

Turn the South Campus woods into a “Living Sustainability Campus” with:

  • Trails
  • Outdoor classrooms
  • Eco-tourism programs

Seek grants, donations, and corporate support for projects on:

  • Climate resilience
  • Biodiversity
  • Education

Host summer programs, conferences, and leadership initiatives that highlight the woods as a unique campus asset instead of cutting it down.
