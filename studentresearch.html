  • Effects of a research-infused botanical Curriculum on Undergraduates’ Content Knowledge, STEM competencies, and Attitudes toward Plant Sciences

Opinion in the Asheville Citizen Times by Dr. Allison Ormsby

Dr. Ormsby has been a biology professor for 25 years and has lived close to the Urban Forest for 21 years. He and his colleagues have conducted outdoor labs and ecological research in the Urban Forest throughout these years, and have been supported by significant extramural funding, hundreds of thousands of dollars from NSF, the NC Biotechnology Center, the McCullough Fellowship, and elsewhere.

Hundreds, if not thousands, of students have participated in these studies. The natural areas around the campus are what make us one of the unique universities, providing an environment for education and research, making it a competitive choice when it comes to biology and environmental science studies.
